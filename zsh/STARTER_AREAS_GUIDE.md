# Good Areas of Responsibility to Get Started

## üéØ What Are Areas of Responsibility?

Areas of Responsibility are ongoing aspects of your life that require maintenance and attention, but don't have a specific end date like projects do. They're the "maintenance" areas that keep your life running smoothly.

**Key difference from projects:**
- **Projects** have a clear outcome and end date
- **Areas** are ongoing and never "complete" - they need regular attention

## üí° How to Use Areas

### 1. Create the Area
```bash
gtd-area create "Health & Wellness"
```

### 2. Add Goals/Standards
Define what "maintained" looks like for this area:
- "Exercise 3-4 times per week"
- "Take medications daily"
- "Eat nutritious meals"

### 3. Review Regularly
During weekly review, check each area:
- What needs attention?
- What's working well?
- What needs improvement?

### 4. Link to Projects
Areas can spawn projects:
- Area: "Health & Wellness"
- Project: "Complete Couch to 5K program"

### 5. Track Progress
Use your daily logs to track area maintenance:
```bash
addInfoToDailyLog "Worked out - kettlebells (Health & Wellness area)"
```

## üîÑ Area Review Process
