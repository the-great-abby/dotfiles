# Life Vision Discovery Guide

## üéØ Overview

Not everyone has a clear life plan or vision‚Äîand that's completely okay! This guide helps you discover what matters to you and create a vision for your life, even if you're starting from scratch.

## üí≠ Why Life Vision Matters

A life vision isn't about having everything figured out. It's about:
- **Direction**: Knowing roughly where you want to go
- **Priorities**: Understanding what matters most to you
- **Decisions**: Having a framework for making choices
- **Motivation**: Having something to work toward

But remember: **Your vision can change!** It's not set in stone.

## üîç Step 1: Start with What You Know

### What Are You Already Doing?

Even without a formal plan, you're already living your life. Start by observing:

#### Questions to Ask Yourself:
1. **What do you spend your time on?**
   - Review your daily logs
   - Look at your calendar
   - Check your projects and tasks
   - What patterns do you see?

2. **What makes you feel good?**
   - When do you feel energized?
   - What activities bring you joy?
   - What makes you feel accomplished?

3. **What do you complain about?**
   - What frustrates you?
   - What would you change if you could?
   - What's missing from your life?

4. **What are you naturally good at?**
   - What comes easily to you?
   - What do people ask you for help with?
   - What skills do you have?

### Use Your GTD System

```bash
# Review your daily logs for patterns
gtd-log view

# Review your projects
gtd-project list

# Review your areas
gtd-area list

# Review your habits
gtd-habit dashboard
```

**Look for:**
- Recurring themes
- Things you keep coming back to
- Areas you invest time in
- What you're already building

## üí° Step 3: Ask Big Questions

### The "Why" Questions

These questions help you discover what matters:

1. **What would make you feel proud?**
   - What accomplishments would you be proud of?
   - What would you want people to say about you?
   - What legacy would you leave?

2. **What would make you happy?**
   - What does happiness look like for you?
   - What would a good day look like?
   - What would a good year look like?

3. **What are you curious about?**
   - What do you want to learn?
   - What interests you?
   - What questions do you have?

4. **What would you do if you weren't afraid?**
   - What risks would you take?
   - What would you try?
   - What would you change?

5. **What would you do if money wasn't a concern?**
   - How would you spend your time?
   - What would you create?
   - What would you contribute?

### The "What If" Questions

1. **What if you had 5 years left?**
   - What would you prioritize?
   - What would you do?
   - What would you change?

2. **What if you could start over?**
   - What would you do differently?
   - What would you keep?
   - What would you change?

3. **What if you couldn't fail?**
   - What would you attempt?
   - What would you create?
   - What would you build?

## üé® Step 4: Create a Vision (Even If It's Vague)

### Start Small

You don't need a 10-year plan. Start with:

#### 1-Year Vision
- What would you like your life to look like in 1 year?
- What would be different?
- What would be the same?

#### 3-Year Vision
- What would you like your life to look like in 3 years?
- What would you have accomplished?
- What would you be working toward?

#### 5-Year Vision
- What would you like your life to look like in 5 years?
- What would you be doing?
- Where would you be?

### Vision Statement Template

**Even if it's vague, write something:**

```
In [timeframe], I want to be someone who:
- [Character trait or value]
- [What you're doing]
- [How you're contributing]

I want my life to include:
- [Important element 1]
- [Important element 2]
- [Important element 3]

I want to feel:
- [Feeling 1]
- [Feeling 2]
```

**Example (even if vague):**
```
In 3 years, I want to be someone who:
- Is healthy and active
- Is learning and growing
- Is making a positive impact

I want my life to include:
- Good relationships
- Work I enjoy
- Time for fun

I want to feel:
- Content
- Challenged
- Connected
```

## üîÑ Step 5: Use Reviews to Refine

### Your Reviews Are Your Discovery Tool

Each review helps you discover more about yourself:

#### Weekly Review
- What went well?
- What didn't?
- What patterns do you see?

#### Monthly Review
- What are you building?
- What's working?
- What needs to change?

#### Quarterly Review
- What are you moving toward?
- What matters most?
- What should you focus on?

#### Yearly Review
- What did you learn about yourself?
- What do you want more of?
- What do you want less of?

### The Discovery Process

**Year 1: Exploration**
- Try different things
- See what sticks
- Learn what you like
- Learn what you don't

**Year 2: Refinement**
- Focus on what works
- Let go of what doesn't
- Build on what matters
- Adjust as you go

**Year 3: Direction**
- You'll start seeing patterns
- You'll know what matters
- You'll have a clearer direction
- You'll refine your vision

**Imagine you're 80 years old, looking back:**
- What would you be proud of?

- What would you want to have done?
- What would you want to have been?

**Start with the end in mind:**
- What would success look like?
- What would failure look like?
- What would you need to do?
- What would you need to learn?
- Who would you need to become?

## üí° Pro Tips

### 1. **It's Okay to Not Know**
- You don't need everything figured out
- Start with what you know
- Build from there
- It's a journey, not a destination

### 2. **Start Small**
- Don't try to plan your entire life
- Start with 1 year
- Expand as you go
- Adjust as you learn

### 3. **Use Your System**
- Your GTD system shows you what matters
- Review your logs
- Look for patterns
- Trust the process

### 4. **Be Flexible**
- Your vision can change
- It should evolve
- Update it regularly
- Don't be rigid

### 5. **Focus on Values, Not Details**
- Values are stable
- Details change
- Focus on what matters
- Let the rest emerge

### 6. **Action Over Planning**
- Don't overthink it
- Start doing things
- Learn as you go
- Adjust based on experience

### 7. **Ask for Help**
- Talk to people you trust
- Get different perspectives
- Consider working with a coach
- Join communities
