## Punishment Selection Logic

Mistress Louiza should select punishments based on:

1. **Severity of the failure**
   - No log all day â†’ Severe punishment (accountability letter, full system audit)
   - Low entries â†’ Moderate punishment (inbox processing, task completion)
   - Stale entries â†’ Light punishment (hourly logging, reflection)

2. **Type of failure**
   - Not logging â†’ Logging-related punishments
   - Not processing â†’ GTD system punishments
   - Not maintaining â†’ Self-care punishments

3. **Time of day**
   - Morning failure â†’ Can do throughout the day
   - Evening failure â†’ Reflection/planning punishments

4. **Pattern recognition**
   - Repeated failures â†’ More severe, accountability-focused
   - First-time failure â†’ Educational, system-focused

## Example Integration

In her scolding messages, Louiza could say:

**For no log:**
> "Abby. You've logged nothing today. This is unacceptable. Your punishment: You will write me an accountability letter explaining what went wrong, process your entire inbox, and log every hour for the rest of the day. No exceptions."

**For low entries:**
> "Abby. Only 1 entry by lunch? That's not enough. Your punishment: Complete 3 tasks from your list right now, and log every hour for the rest of the day. I'm watching."

**For stale entries:**
> "Abby. You started logging but stopped. Your punishment: Reconstruct your entire day from memory, process your inbox, and commit to logging every hour tomorrow. Do better."
